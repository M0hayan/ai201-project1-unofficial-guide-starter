Source: Watterson Towers Official Guide
URL: https://housing.illinoisstate.edu/locations/watterson/

SECTION: Overview
Watterson Towers is a 28-story residence hall and is one of the tallest residence halls in the world. It is also the tallest structure between Chicago and St. Louis.

The building is designed in a suite-style layout rather than a traditional corridor-style residence hall. It includes its own dining commons and additional amenities for residents.

SECTION: Dining Options
Watterson Towers includes an on-site Dining Commons and a Subway restaurant. Additional dining options are available on and off campus within close proximity to the residence hall.

SECTION: Study Spaces
Study spaces are available within Watterson Towers, including quiet areas located in the T-Level of both the North and South towers.

SECTION: Laundry Facilities
Laundry rooms are located on the top floor of each tower and are available 24/7.

Laundry is included in the housing cost. Students may use either high-efficiency (HE) or standard detergent.

Machine availability and remaining cycle time can be checked using the LaundryView system at laundryview.com.

SECTION: Room Layout Options
Watterson Towers offers multiple room configurations, including:
- Single rooms
- Super single suites
- Double rooms
- Triple rooms
- Quad rooms in multiple configurations

SECTION: What Students Should Bring
Recommended items for residents include:
- Bedding (extra-long twin)
- Desk lamp (non-halogen)
- Computer and headphones
- Shower shoes and towels
- Bathrobe and bathroom caddy
- First aid kit
- Refrigerator (must not exceed 4.5 cubic feet or 1.5 amps)
- Umbrella, alarm clock, and basic personal items

SECTION: Refrigerator Policy
Students are allowed to bring personal refrigerators as long as they do not exceed size and power limits. University Housing does not provide or rent refrigerators.

SECTION: Floor Designations
Some floors may be designated as all-female, all-male, or all-gender communities. These designations are intended to support students who prefer shared living environments based on community preferences and personal growth.

SECTION: Room Access and Layout Rules
Watterson Towers is divided into multiple “House” sections (A–E North and South). Elevator access depends on room numbering:
- Rooms starting with “3” are accessible via elevator on the corresponding house floor.
- Rooms not starting with “3” require stair access between floors.

Example: A student in Room C-North 203 must take the elevator to the third floor and then walk down one level.

Staff are available during move-in to assist students with navigation.

SECTION: Elevator System and Modernization Project
Watterson Towers is undergoing an Elevator Modernization Project that began in Spring 2024.

The project includes upgrades to elevator controllers, mechanical systems, and interior components to improve reliability and reduce downtime.

Key points:
- Work is being completed in phases over approximately two years
- One or more elevators may be out of service at any time
- The project is designed to minimize disruption during move-in periods
- Modernization aims to improve long-term reliability and durability

SECTION: Elevator Timeline
- Elevator 1: Mar 2024 – Oct 2024 (completed)
- Elevator 2: Oct 2024 – Mar 2025 (completed/near completion depending on status updates)
- Elevators 5 & 6: Mar 2025 – Aug 2025
- Elevators 3 & 4: Aug 2025 – Feb 2026
- Elevators 7 & 8: Feb 2026 – Jul 2026

SECTION: Current Elevator Status
- Elevators 1, 2, 5, 6: Operational or completed modernization
- Elevators 3, 4: Currently undergoing modernization (expected completion Feb 2026)
- Elevators 7, 8: Not yet started but operational

SECTION: Elevator Limitations
Due to the architectural design of Watterson Towers, elevators do not stop on every floor. This is a structural limitation and cannot be changed through modernization.

SECTION: Summary
The Elevator Modernization Project is intended to improve long-term service reliability while maintaining operational elevators during construction. Some inconvenience may occur due to phased upgrades and partial elevator availability.
