Illinois State University Housing Contract Terms and Conditions (2026–2027)

Introduction

The University Housing Contract is a legally binding agreement covering residence halls (including Hewett Hall, Manchester Hall, Watterson Towers, Haynie, Wright, and Wilkins) and apartment communities (Cardinal Court and Fell/School Street apartments).

By submitting a housing contract, students agree to all terms, including policies posted on the University Housing Services website.

The University may terminate housing if it cannot provide adequate accommodations due to emergencies, capacity limits, or events beyond its control.

Eligibility & Housing Requirements

Students must be registered at Illinois State University and at least 17 years old to live in University Housing.

Full-time status required for priority housing (12+ credits undergrad, 9+ graduate)
University may deny housing based on safety or disciplinary concerns
First- and second-year students are generally required to live on campus unless exempted
Students must remain registered; otherwise housing may be canceled
General Terms
Purpose

Housing is intended for residential and educational use only.

Furnishings

University provides basic furniture (bed, desk, chair, dresser, closet/wardrobe). Students provide personal items such as bedding and lamps.

Furniture cannot be moved between rooms or altered.

Assignment & Room Changes

The University controls room assignments and may:

Assign or reassign rooms
Consolidate vacancies
Require room changes when necessary

Room changes are restricted after initial selection periods.

Assignments are based on gender identity, not biological sex.

Housing Spaces & Occupancy

The University may:

Maintain or adjust room occupancy levels
Consolidate students into shared spaces
Offer reduced occupancy upgrades (if available)
Accommodations

Students may request housing accommodations for disabilities or religious reasons through Student Access and Accommodation Services.

Cancellation Policy (Summary)

Housing contracts follow structured cancellation rules:

Cancellation charges depend on timing
Students must vacate within 3 days of termination
Nonpayment may result in cancellation
Appeals are allowed for extenuating circumstances
Keys & Access
Keys and IDs are issued for assigned spaces only
Duplication or sharing is prohibited
Lost keys may result in lock/core changes billed to student
Students must check in properly to maintain building access
Damage & Liability

Students are responsible for:

Damages to rooms or common areas
Maintenance issues they fail to report
Cleaning conditions at checkout

Shared damages may be split among residents.

The University is not liable for personal property loss.

Mail & Packages

The University is not responsible for lost or undelivered mail.

Solicitation

Sales and solicitation are prohibited without University approval.

Appliances & Prohibited Items

Restrictions include:

No cooking appliances in residence halls (except approved items)
Refrigerators allowed within size/amp limits
No candles, halogen lamps, weapons, pets (except approved animals)
No hoverboards, scooters, or lithium-battery transport devices
Housing Break Closures
Most halls close during breaks
Exceptions: Hewett, Manchester, Wilkins, Cardinal Court, Fell/School Street remain open
Students must live in open buildings for break coverage
Meal Plans
Required for residence hall students
Optional for apartment residents
Automatically assigned if not selected
Room Entry & Inspection

University staff may enter rooms for:

Maintenance
Safety checks
Policy enforcement
Emergencies
Inspections or legal compliance
Health & Safety Policies

Students must follow all public health rules, which may include:

Vaccination/testing requirements
Mask mandates
Quarantine or isolation procedures
Relocation if necessary

Housing costs remain unchanged during health-related disruptions.

Termination of Housing

The University may terminate housing for:

Policy violations
Safety concerns
Disruptive behavior
Noncompliance with directives

Students may appeal termination decisions.

Additional Policies
Equal opportunity policies apply
Housing does not discriminate based on protected characteristics
Students must follow all university and state laws
