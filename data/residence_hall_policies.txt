Accountability Standards
- Any violation of university regulations or residence hall policies may result in disciplinary action.
- Residents are responsible for all information published by University Housing Services.
- Behavior must not interfere with University Housing Services’ educational goals.
- Residents witnessing a violation should remove themselves and report it to staff.
- Housing staff will investigate disruptive behavior; reporting assistance is encouraged.
- Staff members are not eligible for rewards related to reporting incidents.

Housing Behavior Policies

Access
- Residence halls are secured 24/7; Redbird ID required for entry.
- Staff may verify ID at entry points.

Keys and Identification
- Residents must carry ID and room key(s) at all times.
- Watterson residents may require multiple keys (room + suite/hall access).
- Keys must not be shared, duplicated, or altered.
- Lost keys may result in replacement or core change fees.

Spare Keys and Core Changes
- Emergency core change: $150 per lost key.
- Standard core change: $75 per lost key.
- Spare keys must be returned by 8:30 a.m. next business day or fees apply.
- Repeated spare key usage results in additional charges.

Alcohol and Drugs
- Alcohol and illegal/controlled substances (including cannabis) are prohibited in residence halls and apartments.

Decorations in Public Areas
- Must be fire-resistant and not block exits or safety equipment.

Doors
- Inside door decorations limited to 20% coverage.
- Outside door postings limited and regulated by Housing Staff approval.

Energy Conservation / Electrical Appliances
Allowed:
- Hair tools, mirrors, shavers, irons (designated areas only)
- Single-serve coffee makers (e.g., Keurig)

Prohibited:
- Open flames, halogen lamps, lava lamps, torchiere lamps
- Water boilers, electric kettles, oil popcorn poppers
- Hoverboards, electric scooters, powered tools

Violations may result in fines ($50 first, $100 subsequent).

Elevators
- Available in all residence halls.
- Damage costs are billed to residents if responsible party is unknown.
- Emergency misuse or damage may result in fines or criminal charges.

Escort and Visitation
- Guests must be escorted at all times.
- Residents are responsible for guest behavior.
- Maximum of 3 guests per resident.
- Guests may stay up to 72 hours.
- Guest registration is required 24/7.

Mail Services
- Mail delivered daily (Monday–Saturday).
- Residents must update address via university system.
- Mailbox misuse or damage may result in penalties.

Maintenance Requests
- Requests submitted via iService Desk.
- Emergency issues should be reported to front desk.
- Failure to report damage may result in billing.

Noise Policy
Courtesy Hours:
- 24/7 courtesy expectation

Quiet Hours:
- Sunday–Thursday: 11 p.m. – 7 a.m.
- Friday–Saturday: 12 a.m. – 7 a.m.

Guidelines:
- Noise must not disturb others.
- Headphones encouraged.
- Residents must resolve conflicts respectfully.
- Staff may intervene if needed.

Finals Week Quiet Hours
- Extended quiet hours (20–24 hrs/day)
- Violations may result in relocation or housing removal.

Pets
- Only fish allowed (max 10-gallon tank).
- All other pets prohibited.

Room Change Policy
- Allowed after first 10 days of classes.
- Must be approved by Residence Hall Coordinator.
- Unauthorized changes are violations.

Room Condition & Cleanliness
- No accumulation of trash or perishables.
- Limited recycling container allowance.

Smoke and Tobacco Policy
- Smoking and vaping prohibited on campus.

Sports in Halls
- No sports or recreational physical activity indoors.

Sprinkler Systems
- Must not be obstructed or tampered with.
- Damage may result in fines and liability.

Student Room Policies
Furniture:
- University-provided furniture only.
- No disassembly or removal allowed.

Decorations:
- No attachments to lights, ceilings, or sprinklers.
- Wall coverage limited to 20%.
- LED adhesive use that damages walls is prohibited.

Open Flame:
- Candles, incense, wax warmers, etc. are prohibited.

Trash
- Must use designated trash chutes.
- Hazardous waste must be handled separately.

Safety Policies

Fire Safety:
- Immediate evacuation required when alarms sound.
- Fire alarm violations may result in suspension or charges.

Emergency Equipment:
- Tampering with alarms, sprinklers, or fire equipment prohibited.

Harassment:
- Discrimination and harassment prohibited in all forms.
- Includes verbal, physical, digital, and sexual harassment.
- Must be reported to ISU Police or Equal Opportunity Office.

Room Entry and Search
- University may enter rooms for safety, maintenance, or policy enforcement.
- Personal belongings are not searched unless in plain sight violation.

Room Occupancy Limits
Maximum occupancy depends on room type:

Watterson Towers:
- Singles: 6
- Doubles: 13
- Deluxe Doubles: 20

Hewett & Manchester:
- Singles: 8
- Doubles: 11
- Deluxe Doubles: 20

Tri-Towers:
- Singles: 8
- Doubles: 11
- Deluxe Doubles: 20

Weapons
- Weapons (including replicas and certain knives) are prohibited.
- Violations may result in removal, suspension, or criminal charges.

Window Policy
- Screens may not be removed.
- Nothing may be thrown or dropped from windows.
- Violations may result in fines up to $200 per incident.

Emergency Procedures

Fire Procedure
- Call 911 immediately.
- Evacuate during alarms.
- If trapped, stay low and signal for help.

Illness/Injury
Contact:
- Housing staff
- Front desks
- Student Health Services
- ISU Police
- 911

Tornado Procedure
- Tornado Watch: be alert and prepared.
- Tornado Warning: move to designated shelter immediately.
- Follow staff and emergency alert instructions.
